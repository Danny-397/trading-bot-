HOW IT WORKS

An algorithmic trading bot running on Alpaca's paper-trading environment — no real money ever at risk. Built from scratch by Danny as an independent CS project exploring quantitative finance.

TRADING STRATEGIES

Strategy 1 — Moving Average Crossover

The most fundamental algorithmic trading strategy. It computes two simple moving averages of a stock's closing price — a fast (20-day) and a slow (50-day) — and trades when they cross.

▲BUY signal: 20-day SMA crosses above 50-day SMA (golden cross) — momentum is turning upward.
▼SELL signal: 20-day SMA crosses below 50-day SMA (death cross) — momentum is turning downward.

Maths: SMA(n) = average of the last n closing prices. Crossover = sign change of (SMA20 − SMA50).

Strategy 2 — RSI Mean Reversion

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) is a momentum oscillator ranging 0–100. Extreme values signal that a stock is temporarily over- or under-valued relative to recent history.

▲BUY signal: RSI drops below 30 — stock is oversold, likely to bounce back up.
▼SELL signal: RSI rises above 70 — stock is overbought, likely to pull back.

Maths: RSI = 100 − 100 / (1 + RS), where RS = average gain / average loss over 14 periods.

Strategy 3 — MACD Momentum

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence indicator compares two exponential moving averages. A signal line smooths the MACD line; their crossover indicates momentum shifts. Volume confirmation filters out false signals.

▲BUY signal: MACD line crosses above signal line and volume is above its 20-day average.
▼SELL signal: MACD line crosses below signal line (no volume filter on exits).

Maths: MACD = EMA(12) − EMA(26). Signal = EMA(9) of MACD. Histogram = MACD − Signal.

RISK MANAGEMENT

Every trade placed by the bot must pass through the risk module first. These rules are non-negotiable — they execute on every single order.

RULE VALUE WHY IT MATTERS
Stop-loss −5% Automatically sells if a position falls 5% below entry price, capping losses.
Take-profit +15% Locks in gains by selling when a position is up 15% — prevents giving profits back.
Max position size 10% of portfolio No single stock can represent more than 10% of the total account, forcing diversification.
Cash reserve 20% minimum Always keeps 20% of portfolio as cash, preventing full exposure and enabling new opportunities.
Daily trade cap 10 orders/day Limits overtrading, reducing transaction costs and emotional over-reaction to noise.
Market hours 9:30 AM – 4:00 PM EST Only trades during regular NYSE/NASDAQ hours, avoiding illiquid pre/after-market conditions.

ARCHITECTURE

The project is a clean two-tier app: a Python/Flask backend and a vanilla JS/HTML frontend.

  • app.py — Flask REST API exposing all data and control endpoints.
  • bot.py — Runs a background thread that wakes every 5 minutes, checks risk gates, then generates signals and submits orders to Alpaca.
  • strategies.py — Pure Python indicator maths (SMA, RSI, MACD) implemented from the formulas, no black-box libraries.
  • backtest.py — Daily-bar simulation engine with identical risk rules to the live bot. Downloads historical data from yfinance and walks through it day-by-day.
  • risk.py — Centralised risk gate: market-hours check, daily trade counter, position sizing, stop/take-profit calculation.
  • database.py — SQLite persistence layer storing every trade, daily snapshots, and bot state. Metrics survive restarts.
  • frontend/ — Three vanilla HTML pages, Chart.js for equity curves, auto-refreshes every 10 seconds via the REST API.

DEFAULT WATCHLIST

The bot monitors seven large-cap US equities by default: AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, GOOGL, TSLA, JPM, SPY. These were chosen for high liquidity, low spread, and diverse sector exposure.

SETUP

  1. Get a free Alpaca account at alpaca.markets and generate paper-trading API keys.
  2. Copy .env.example to .env and fill in your keys.
  3. Install Python dependencies: pip install -r backend/requirements.txt
  4. Start the backend: python backend/app.py
  5. Open frontend/index.html in a browser (or serve with any static host).
